# Support Instructor
**Amman Cybersecurity & Digital Forensics Program**
📍 Amman, Jordan · 16 Weeks (2025–2026) · Contract

---

## About the Program

The Amman Cybersecurity & Digital Forensics Program is a 16-week dual-track hybrid training program serving 100 learners across cybersecurity (75) and digital forensics (25) specializations. The program runs three cohorts through a shared foundation, specialized tracks, and a collaborative applied capstone.

---

## Role Overview

The Support Instructor is the onsite technical lead. You translate conceptual instruction into applied execution, maintain pacing and quality, supervise TAs, and ensure learner readiness week over week.

You deliver two lab sessions per week per track and oversee capstone projects in the program's final phase. You are the bridge between curriculum design and real learner output.

---

## Position Within the Instructional System

| Role | Owns |
|------|------|
| **Lead Instructor** | Curriculum architecture, conceptual depth, and final academic judgment |
| **Support Instructor** | Applied execution, lab delivery, grading oversight, TA management, and learner readiness enforcement |
| **Teaching Assistants** | Daily debugging support, first-pass grading, and early risk detection |

Final academic judgment rests with the Lead Instructor. However, you hold clear authority over lab execution quality, grading calibration, readiness enforcement, and TA supervision.

---

## What You'll Do

### Lab Delivery (2 sessions/week per track)
- Demonstrate new technical components, translate abstract concepts into working systems, guide learners through structured implementation, and model debugging live
- Reinforce reproducibility and documentation standards in every session
- Manage pacing so faster learners are challenged and slower learners are supported without lowering standards

### Debugging & Office Hours
- Teach learners to interpret error messages, reinforce logging and testing discipline, ask guiding questions rather than providing solutions, and model structured problem decomposition
- Coordinate TA coverage during work blocks — ensure no learner remains blocked for more than one day
- Surface systemic technical issues to the Lead Instructor immediately

### Grading Oversight & Quality Control
- Review and approve reference solutions; ensure alignment between assignments and learning outcomes
- Audit TA rubric application and provide second-level review when needed
- Escalate grading disputes to the Lead Instructor
- Turnaround standards: ~48 hours for drills and labs; ~72 hours for items requiring instructor review

### TA Supervision & Calibration
- Daily touchpoints with TAs (in-person or async)
- Monitor grading backlog, ensure rubric alignment, coach TAs on feedback tone and standards
- Delegate workload strategically; correct grading inconsistencies immediately
- Ensure TAs are always prioritizing struggling learners over advanced ones

### Learner Readiness Monitoring
- Monitor readiness across four pillars: Technical Readiness, Timeliness & Professional Rhythm, Engagement, and Integrity & Explainability
- Initiate early interventions for learners at risk
- Escalate to the Lead Instructor within 24 hours when: integrity issues arise, multiple readiness failures appear, or attendance thresholds are approached
- You are accountable for preventing silent failure

### Capstone Oversight (Weeks 13–16)
- Guide teams through project scoping, architecture design, technical feasibility, reproducible deployment, and presentation readiness
- Verify that each capstone repository runs cleanly on a fresh machine, has documented setup steps, and demonstrates coherent system design
- Provide structured feedback prior to final evaluation
- Co-deliver applied capstone alongside both lead instructors

### Curriculum Review
- Beginning each Sunday, dedicate 60–90 minutes per day to reviewing module materials due the following Sunday
- Check instruction clarity, assignment flow, and difficulty levels — flag any blocking issues to the Lead Instructor immediately
- Feedback is due one full week before delivery begins — do not leave it until the deadline

### Team Coordination & Reporting
- Daily or frequent syncs with TAs
- Weekly syncs with the Lead Instructor
- Clear communication with the Program/Project Manager on scheduling and logistics
- Surface immediately: learner performance data, grading backlog, technical blockers, cohort morale concerns

## What You Bring

- Instructional or facilitation experience in a technical training environment
- Solid working knowledge across both cybersecurity and digital forensics fundamentals — you don't need to be a deep specialist in one track, but you need to bridge both
- Ability to hold authority over lab execution, grading calibration, and TA supervision with decisiveness, clarity, and accountability
- Strong experience in hybrid delivery environments
- Familiarity with the Security+/CySA+/CASP+ cert pathway and MITRE ATT&CK framework
- Calm, organized presence — the first two weeks and the capstone phase both depend on your ability to hold the cohort together
