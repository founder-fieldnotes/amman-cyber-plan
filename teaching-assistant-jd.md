# Teaching Assistant (TA)
**Amman Cybersecurity & Digital Forensics Program**
📍 Amman, Jordan · 16 Weeks (2025–2026) · Contract

---

## About the Program

The Amman Cybersecurity & Digital Forensics Program is a 16-week dual-track hybrid training program serving 100 learners across cybersecurity (75) and digital forensics (25) specializations. The program runs three cohorts — two cybersecurity, one digital forensics — through a shared foundation, specialized tracks, and a collaborative applied capstone.

---

## Role Overview

TAs are a critical execution layer in this program. You are not secondary support — you are the daily force that maintains learner momentum, readiness standards, and professional discipline. The program requires a minimum of 3 TAs, with 4–5 preferred, active from Weeks 3–12.

You are the early warning system. If someone quietly falls behind, the program fails.

---

## What You'll Do

### Every Day
- Check the student dashboard to identify learners of concern — never rely on what a student says they completed; always verify first
- Group learners by issue type and run targeted support sessions proactively — do not wait to be asked
- Brief sync after each session; log notes and escalate blockers to the Support Instructor same day

### Lab & Assignment Support
- Attend lab sessions, observe where learners struggle, and run targeted support after the Support Instructor completes the demo
- Ensure every learner submits an attempt before end of each lab day — even an incomplete one; attempts must be on record
- Prioritize learners who still need to complete assignments, working with the highest-need students first

### Grading
- Conduct first-pass grading of labs, integration tasks, peer reviews, and reflection artifacts
- Manually evaluate: screenshot authenticity, commit message quality, PR description completeness, README documentation, and rubric alignment
- Meet turnaround standards: ~48 hours for drills and labs; escalate integration tasks to the Support Instructor within 72 hours
- Tone standard: Direct. Precise. Professional. Not praise-heavy. Not vague.

### Debugging & Coaching
- When learners are stuck: ask them to show the failing line or error, guide interpretation of the error message, and suggest the next diagnostic step
- Do not write their solution or paste corrected code — the goal is building independence

### Risk Detection
- Process reflection exports and act on flagged learners within 24 hours — reflection flags are predictive indicators, act early
- Escalate integrity concerns to the Support Instructor before making any learner outreach

### Curriculum Review
- Each Sunday, open module materials scheduled for two weeks out and begin testing and leaving feedback on instruction clarity, assignment flow, and difficulty levels
- Feedback is due no later than one full week before delivery begins
- Target: 60–90 minutes per day throughout the week

---

## What You Are NOT Responsible For
- Re-running automated checks manually
- Modifying curriculum content
- Making unilateral academic integrity judgments
- Overriding rubric standards

---

## What You Bring

- Hands-on technical experience in cybersecurity, digital forensics, or a closely related field
- Familiarity with tools relevant to your track (e.g., Nmap, ELK/Wazuh, Volatility, Autopsy, Metasploit)
- Ability to enforce professional habits: clean repo structure, meaningful commit messages, proper branching, clear README files, and reproducibility standards
- Strong communication skills and comfort working in hybrid environments
- Organized and proactive — you seek out struggling learners rather than waiting to be flagged
- Familiarity with the Security+/CySA+ pathway and MITRE ATT&CK framework (preferred)

## Schedule & Presence

Core hours are Monday–Thursday in a hybrid format. Sunday concept sessions are virtual for all learners. The digital forensics cohort receives priority in-person access during hardware-dependent weeks; cybersecurity cohorts rotate in-person participation for guided labs and applied work.

Reliability is essential. Learners stall if support disappears.

---

## Standard of Conduct

Model professional behavior at all times. You are not grading students — you are preparing future colleagues. TAs are expected to operate with clarity, consistency, and accountability.
