Responsibilities

What you'll do

Lab delivery, grading oversight, TA supervision, readiness monitoring, capstone oversight, and curriculum review — across the full 16 weeks.
Lab delivery

2 sessions / week / track

  • Demonstrate new technical components, translate abstract concepts into working systems, and guide learners through structured implementation
  • Model debugging live — reinforce reproducibility and documentation standards in every session
  • Manage pacing: faster learners challenged, slower learners supported without lowering standards
  • Hand off to TAs after the demo; supervise their support without taking over
  • Confirm all learners have submitted an attempt before end of each lab day
Grading oversight

Consistency & standards

  • Review and approve reference solutions; ensure alignment between assignments and learning outcomes
  • Audit TA rubric application; provide second-level review when needed
  • Escalate grading disputes to the Lead Instructor
  • ~48 hours for drills and labs; ~72 hours for items requiring instructor review
Grading consistency matters more than speed. If inconsistency appears, correct it immediately — don't wait for the weekly sync.
TA supervision

Daily calibration

  • Daily touchpoints with TAs — in-person or async
  • Monitor grading backlog; ensure rubric alignment
  • Coach TAs on feedback tone and standards
  • Delegate workload strategically; correct inconsistencies immediately
  • Ensure TAs are always prioritizing struggling learners over advanced ones
Learner readiness

Prevent silent failure

  • Monitor readiness across four pillars: Technical Readiness, Timeliness & Professional Rhythm, Engagement, and Integrity & Explainability
  • Initiate early interventions for learners at risk — never wait for the problem to compound
  • Escalate to the Lead Instructor within 24 hours when integrity issues arise, multiple readiness failures appear, or attendance thresholds are approached
  • You are accountable for preventing silent failure.
Capstone oversight · Wks 13–16

Applied project delivery

  • Guide teams through project scoping, architecture design, technical feasibility, and reproducible deployment
  • Verify each capstone repository runs cleanly on a fresh machine, has documented setup steps, and demonstrates coherent system design
  • Provide structured feedback prior to final evaluation
  • Co-deliver applied capstone alongside both lead instructors
Scoping Architecture review Reproducibility check Presentation readiness
Curriculum review

One week ahead, every week

  • Beginning each Sunday, dedicate 60–90 minutes per day to reviewing module materials due the following Sunday
  • Check instruction clarity, assignment flow, and difficulty levels
  • Flag any blocking issues to the Lead Instructor immediately — do not wait until the deadline
  • Feedback is due one full week before delivery begins
60–90 min/day GitHub Classroom Due: 1 wk out
Debugging standard

How you run office hours

Structured debugging blocks run weekly. Your role is to teach learners to solve problems, not to solve them on their behalf.
Your debugging standard

Teach, don't fix

  • Teach learners to interpret error messages — walk them through the message before suggesting a fix
  • Reinforce logging and testing discipline during every debugging session
  • Ask guiding questions rather than providing solutions; model structured problem decomposition
  • Coordinate TA coverage during work blocks — ensure no learner remains blocked for extended periods
  • Surface systemic technical issues to the Lead Instructor immediately. Do not absorb recurring blockers silently.
Team coordination

Reporting cadence

  • Daily or frequent syncs with TAs
  • Weekly syncs with the Lead Instructor
  • Clear communication with Program/Project Manager on scheduling

Surface immediately:

  • Learner performance data
  • Grading backlog
  • Technical blockers
  • Cohort morale concerns
