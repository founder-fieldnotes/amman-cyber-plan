Responsibilities

What you'll do

You own the execution layer of the program: delivery, grading oversight, TA supervision, readiness monitoring, learner coaching, capstone support, and forward-looking curriculum review.
Applied delivery

Lab + integration execution

  • Deliver two applied sessions per week per track: the Applied Lab and the Integration Task
  • Demonstrate new technical components and translate abstract concepts into working systems
  • Guide learners through structured implementation and model debugging live
  • Reinforce reproducibility, documentation quality, and engineering discipline in every session
  • Manage pacing so stronger learners stay challenged while slower learners are supported without lowering standards
Grading oversight

Consistency & standards

  • Review and approve reference solutions and ensure alignment between assignments and outcomes
  • Audit TA rubric application and provide second-level review when needed
  • Escalate grading disputes to the Lead Instructor
  • Ensure feedback reinforces reproducibility, technical defensibility, documentation quality, and engineering discipline
  • Maintain expected turnaround: about 48 hours for drills and labs, about 72 hours for items needing instructor review
Grading consistency matters more than speed. If inconsistency appears, correct it immediately rather than letting it spread.
TA supervision

Daily calibration

  • Run daily touchpoints with TAs, in person or async
  • Monitor grading backlog and ensure rubric alignment
  • Coach TAs on standards, tone, delegation, and priority-setting
  • Step in where needed without removing TA ownership of their work
  • Ensure highest-need learners are always prioritized ahead of advanced learners
Readiness monitoring

Prevent silent failure

  • Monitor learner readiness across four pillars: Technical Readiness, Timeliness & Professional Rhythm, Engagement, and Integrity & Explainability
  • Initiate early interventions when learners are slipping, blocked, or showing weak professional rhythm
  • Escalate to the Lead Instructor within 24 hours when integrity issues arise, multiple readiness failures appear, or attendance thresholds are approached
  • You are accountable for catching problems early.
Capstone oversight

Applied project support

  • Guide teams through scoping, architecture design, technical feasibility, and reproducible deployment
  • Verify each capstone repository runs cleanly on a fresh machine and includes clear setup steps
  • Provide structured feedback before final evaluation
  • Support presentation readiness and technical coherence across teams
Scoping Architecture Reproducibility Presentation readiness
Curriculum review

One week ahead, every week

  • Beginning each Sunday, dedicate 60–90 minutes per day to reviewing materials due the following Sunday
  • Open the module two weeks ahead and begin testing, checking instruction clarity, assignment flow, and difficulty
  • Flag blocking issues immediately rather than waiting for the deadline
  • Submit feedback one full week before delivery begins
60–90 min/day GitHub Classroom 1 week ahead
Debugging standard

How you run office hours

Structured debugging blocks run weekly. The goal is not to solve problems for learners, but to teach them how to reason through problems with discipline.
Your debugging standard

Teach, don't fix

  • Teach learners to interpret error messages before suggesting fixes
  • Reinforce logging, testing, and structured problem decomposition
  • Ask guiding questions rather than providing answers too early
  • Coordinate TA coverage so blocked learners do not sit unattended for extended periods
  • Surface systemic technical issues to the Lead Instructor immediately.
Team coordination

Reporting cadence

  • Daily or frequent syncs with TAs
  • Weekly syncs with the Lead Instructor
  • Clear communication with the Program/Project Manager on scheduling and logistics

Surface immediately:

  • Learner performance data
  • Grading backlog
  • Technical blockers
  • Cohort morale concerns

Qualifications

What you bring

This role requires instructional credibility, operational calm, and the ability to bridge technical content into applied execution. The standard is visible leadership, not passive support.
Required

Core qualifications

  • Instructional or facilitation experience in a technical training environment
  • Working knowledge of cybersecurity and digital forensics fundamentals, or the ability to bridge adjacent domains with confidence
  • Ability to hold authority over lab execution, grading calibration, and TA supervision with clarity and accountability
  • Strong experience in hybrid or multi-layer instructional environments
  • Comfort with structured debugging, technical coaching, and enforcing professional engineering behavior
  • Calm, organized presence under pressure

Standard of conduct

How you operate

  • Decisiveness — act when you have enough information, escalate when you do not
  • Visibility — be present, reachable, and predictable
  • Accountability — own lab quality, TA calibration, and learner readiness without waiting to be asked
  • Professional rigor — reinforce reproducibility, documentation, branching hygiene, and responsible AI use
Reliability and visible presence are essential. The cohort stalls when support disappears.
