Operating model

How this role fits into delivery

The Core Concept Speaker works from Lead-approved material, then hands the week cleanly into the applied delivery team. This role gives the cohort conceptual alignment without duplicating the Lead Instructor or Support Instructor responsibilities.
Step 1

Receive the weekly brief

Review approved module goals, key concepts, vocabulary, examples, and learner outcomes for the upcoming shared session.

Step 2

Lead the concept session

Teach the shared cohort live, clarify the logic behind the week, and reinforce documentation, communication, and business-aware thinking.

Step 3

Hand off to labs

Leave Support Instructors with a clean bridge into the week's labs, including emphasis points, risk framing, and likely learner confusion areas.

This role owns clarity, not the whole pipeline. The job is to make the week legible, credible, and memorable before applied execution begins.
Responsibilities

What you'll do

This role is focused, learner-facing, and intentionally lightweight. You are responsible for the shared concept experience that sets the tone for the week, not for the full instructional stack.
Live instruction

Lead the shared session

  • Teach designated virtual concept sessions for the full cohort
  • Explain frameworks, mental models, and weekly operating logic in plain, credible language
  • Use examples that connect directly to the applied labs learners will enter next
  • Keep pacing strong for mixed-experience learners without flattening the technical rigor
Instructional translation

Make the material land

  • Turn approved modules into clear live explanations, walkthroughs, and comparisons
  • Surface where concepts differ across Cybersecurity and Digital Forensics without losing the shared throughline
  • Reinforce vocabulary, conceptual precision, and the "why" behind the tooling
  • Give learners a clean mental map before they touch the applied exercises
Professional habits

Reinforce the right instincts

  • Model clear communication, structured note-taking, and decision-ready explanation
  • Reinforce business-risk framing and mission impact where relevant
  • Keep evidence handling, documentation discipline, and professional judgment visible from the beginning
  • Show learners how strong practitioners explain technical work to different audiences
Coordination

Hand off cleanly

  • Coordinate with the Lead Instructor on content boundaries and emphasis
  • Coordinate with Support Instructors so the concept session tees up the week's labs accurately
  • Flag unclear module language or conceptual gaps before delivery when possible
  • Share post-session notes on learner confusion, questions, and follow-up needs
What we prepare for you

What will already be in place

  • An approved module brief with the core concepts, goals, and intended learner outcomes
  • A clear understanding of where your shared session fits in the weekly sequence
  • Coordination with the Lead Instructor and Support Instructors on emphasis, boundaries, and handoff points
  • The applied labs, TA support, grading workflows, and learner follow-through handled by the rest of the instructional team

Role shape

Designed for specialists

  • This can be a recurring light-touch role or a guest-style role for designated modules
  • The best fit may be a practitioner who teaches especially well, even if they are not in the full delivery chain
  • Subject-matter depth matters, but delivery clarity matters just as much
  • The standard is not just expertise. It is whether learners actually understand the week better because you were there.
Working model

How the role works

This role is designed to be flexible, remote-friendly, and easy to pair with working practitioners. The center of gravity is a brief prep window plus strong live teaching, not constant attendance.
Preparation

Minimal prep, clear delivery

  • Review approved materials ahead of the session in about an hour
  • Prepare one or two examples, diagrams, stories, or comparisons that make the concepts stick
  • Clarify where the shared session ends and the applied lab begins
  • Coordinate with the instructional team on any risks, dependencies, or content sensitivities
Delivery

Live when it matters

  • Join live for the designated concept session and any tightly scoped follow-up needed that week
  • Most work can be handled asynchronously before and after the session
  • This role is compatible with external practitioners, guest experts, and part-time contributors

Qualifications

What you bring

This role is ideal for someone with real technical credibility who can also teach clearly to a mixed cohort without needing heavy prep or ownership of the whole module. Strong communication, judgment, and narrative clarity matter as much as subject-matter depth.
Required

Core qualifications

  • Strong practitioner experience in cybersecurity, digital forensics, incident response, or a closely related field
  • Ability to teach technical concepts clearly to learners who are smart but still building fluency
  • Comfort explaining systems, networking, documentation, and tradeoffs in accessible language
  • Strong speaking presence in live virtual sessions
  • Ability to connect technical content to business risk, operational reality, and professional communication
  • Reliable preparation habits and willingness to coordinate with the instructional team
